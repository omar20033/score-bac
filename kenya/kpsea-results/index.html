How to Check KPSEA Results

What KPSEA is, how to access Grade 6 results on the official KNEC portal, and what the report means for your child's move to Junior School

Always use the official source. The official KPSEA results platform is kpsea.knec.ac.ke. Be wary of third-party sites or unofficial SMS numbers claiming to offer faster or alternative access — verify any shortcode against the official KNEC portal or your child's school before trusting it.

Step-by-step — checking KPSEA results online

  1. Go to the official KPSEA portal

    Open kpsea.knec.ac.ke directly in your browser. Avoid links shared on WhatsApp or social media — type the address yourself or use this page's link.

  2. Enter the Assessment Number

    Type the learner's Assessment Number exactly as it appears on the registration slip from their school.

  3. Enter a registered name

    Enter at least one of the learner's names exactly as it was registered for the assessment.

  4. Accept the privacy notice

    Read and accept the privacy and access notice to proceed to the search.

  5. View and save the report

    Search to view the individual performance report. You can download or print it for your records and to share with the school if needed.

✓ Does KPSEA decide Junior School placement?

No — Kenya runs a 100% transition policy. Every Grade 6 learner who sits KPSEA moves on to Junior School (Grade 7), regardless of their score. KPSEA is diagnostic rather than selective: it's designed to show teachers and the Ministry where learners are strong and where they need more support, not to decide who advances.

The results do, however, carry forward as part of a learner's record: KPSEA performance contributes a share of the eventual composite score used later, when Grade 9 learners sit KJSEA and are guided into Senior School pathways.

📱 Checking KPSEA results by SMS

The KNEC portal is the most reliable way to view a full performance report, but KNEC has also offered SMS shortcodes in past assessment cycles — for example, 20076 has been used to check KPSEA registration status and results, for a small fee per message. The exact shortcode can change between years, so don't rely on a number you saw on social media or a previous year's guide.

Before sending an SMS to any shortcode, confirm the current, correct number through the official KNEC portal, a recent KNEC notice, or directly with your child's school.

How the score is built. A learner's KPSEA report reflects two components: continuous assessment recorded by teachers across Grades 4 to 6, combined with the score from the national assessment KNEC administers at the end of Grade 6. The exact weighting of each component is set by KNEC and reflected directly on the individual performance report, so always refer to your child's own report for the precise breakdown rather than a fixed public formula.

Frequently asked questions

What does KPSEA mean?
KPSEA stands for Kenya Primary School Education Assessment — the national assessment KNEC gives to Grade 6 learners at the end of primary school, under the Competency-Based Curriculum.
How do I check KPSEA results?
Visit the official KNEC portal at kpsea.knec.ac.ke, enter the learner's Assessment Number and at least one registered name, accept the privacy notice, then search to view and download the individual performance report.
Can I check KPSEA results by SMS?
Yes, in past cycles KNEC has offered an SMS option — for example shortcode 20076 — for checking KPSEA registration status and results, at a small cost per message. However, this shortcode can change between assessment years, so always confirm the current, correct number through the official KNEC portal or your child's school rather than relying on an old or unverified number.
How do I check my child's KPSEA placement?
KPSEA does not place or select learners — under the 100% transition policy, all Grade 6 candidates move on to Junior School (Grade 7) regardless of their results. What you can check is the individual performance report, which shows how your child performed by subject and competency area.
When are KPSEA results released?
Results are typically released some weeks after the Grade 6 assessment, held in October, once marking and verification are complete. The exact release date is confirmed publicly by KNEC and the Ministry of Education closer to the time and is not fixed each year.
What subjects are tested in KPSEA?
KPSEA covers Mathematics, English, Kiswahili, Integrated Science, Social Studies, and Creative Arts.
What is KILEA and how is it different from KPSEA?
KILEA (Kenya Intermediate Level Education Assessment) is the equivalent assessment for learners with special needs at the intermediate level. Unlike KPSEA, KILEA results are generally accessed through the candidate's school rather than the public online portal.
