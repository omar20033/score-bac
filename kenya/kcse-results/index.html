How to Check Your KCSE Results

A step-by-step guide to the official KNEC results portal, the full grade-points table, and answers to the questions candidates ask most

Always use the official source. The only official KCSE results platform is the KNEC portal at results.knec.ac.ke. KNEC and the Ministry of Education have repeatedly warned candidates about fake websites and social media posts claiming to offer faster or alternative access to results — these are not legitimate and are often designed to collect personal data or charge unofficial fees.

Step-by-step — checking your results on the KNEC portal

  1. Go to the official portal

    Open results.knec.ac.ke directly in your browser. Don't follow links shared on WhatsApp or social media — type the address yourself or use this page's link.

  2. Select the examination year

    Choose the correct KCSE examination year from the portal's selection field before entering your details.

  3. Enter your index number

    Type your full KCSE index number exactly as printed on your examination admission slip, including all digits.

  4. Enter your registered name

    Enter one of your names exactly as it was registered for the exam. Spelling must match your registration data.

  5. Accept and submit

    Accept the privacy and access notice, then submit to view your provisional results slip. You can print or screenshot it for your records.

📱 Checking KCSE results by SMS

SMS access to KCSE results has been inconsistent between release cycles. In some years KNEC has supported checking results by sending your index number to shortcode 20076, at a small cost per message on Safaricom, Airtel, and Telkom. In other years, reports have indicated the SMS option was discontinued in favour of the online portal only.

Because availability changes year to year, treat the KNEC portal as the reliable channel, and only use SMS if KNEC's own release-day announcement confirms it's active for that year. Don't trust a shortcode shared on social media without that confirmation.

Grade boundaries for the mean grade can shift slightly each year based on KNEC's national grading review, so treat the bands above as a general guide rather than a fixed cutoff.

What the symbols on your result slip mean

W

Withheld. Usually linked to a suspected case of examination malpractice that is still under investigation by KNEC.

U

Ungraded. The candidate sat the paper but did not meet the minimum criteria required for a grade to be awarded.

X

Absent. Commonly used where a candidate did not sit a particular paper they were registered for.

P

Pending. Indicates the result is still being processed or verified before it can be confirmed.

Found an error on your slip? Genuine errors — such as a missing subject, wrong index details, or a miscalculated total — can usually be corrected. Corrections are normally initiated through your school's examination officer, typically within 30 days of the results release, after which a processing fee may apply. Contact your school first.

Frequently asked questions

How do I check my KCSE results?
Visit the official KNEC portal at results.knec.ac.ke, select the exam year, then enter your index number and one of your registered names exactly as they appear on your admission slip.
Can I check KCSE results by SMS?
Sometimes — KNEC has supported an SMS option (commonly shortcode 20076) in some release cycles, but it hasn't been available every year and reporting on its current status is often mixed. The KNEC portal is the reliable channel each year; only use SMS if it's confirmed active in that year's official release announcement.
When are KCSE results released?
Results are traditionally released in January, following the KCSE sitting in October–November of the previous year. The exact date is confirmed by the Ministry of Education and KNEC a short time before release and is not set on a fixed annual date.
What grade is 45 points in KCSE?
A total of 45 out of 84 points across your best 7 subjects generally falls in the B- to B mean grade range. Exact boundaries can move slightly year to year, so use the grade table above as a general guide and confirm your specific mean grade on your official result slip.
What does W mean on a KCSE result slip?
W stands for withheld — typically because KNEC is investigating a suspected case of examination malpractice linked to that result.
What does U mean on a KCSE result slip?
U stands for ungraded — the candidate sat the exam paper, but the performance did not meet the minimum threshold required for a grade to be issued.
Can I correct an error on my KCSE results?
Yes, for genuine errors such as a missing subject or incorrect registration detail. Start the correction process through your school's examination officer, ideally within 30 days of the results release, since queries lodged later may attract a processing fee.
Where do I collect my official KCSE certificate?
Certificate collection has shifted over the years — in some recent cycles it has moved from individual schools to sub-county education offices. Confirm the current collection point with your school or your local sub-county education office, since this detail can change between cycles.
