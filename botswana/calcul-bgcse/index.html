Frequently Asked Questions — BGCSE & JCE Points

How do you calculate BGCSE points?
Each BGCSE grade is converted to a point value on the scale most widely used for admissions purposes (A* and A = 8, B = 7, C = 6, D = 5, E = 4, F = 3, G = 2). Add the points for your best six subjects to get your total, out of a maximum of 48 points.

Example: A* (8) + A (8) + B (7) + B (7) + C (6) + C (6) = 42 points.

Important: BEC itself describes BGCSE as a syllabus-based qualification that does not officially aggregate. The points conversion above is an admissions convention used by universities and the Department of Tertiary Education Financing (DTEF) — it does not appear on your official BEC statement of results.
What is the JCE aggregate and how is it different from BGCSE?
JCE (Junior Certificate Examination) uses a numeric aggregate out of a maximum of 63, based on your best six subjects — this is a different system from BGCSE's points-per-grade scale.

Grade bands: Merit (90+) · A (80–89) · B (60–79) · C (50–59) · D (30–49) · E (26–29) · below 26 is ungraded (U).
What BGCSE points do you need for University of Botswana (UB)?
UB's general minimum entry requirement is six BGCSE subjects not below grade E, with at least five subjects at grade C or better in one sitting, English Language as one of the qualifying subjects (minimum C for non-science programmes, minimum D for science-based programmes), and a minimum of 34 points for degree programmes or 30 points for diploma/certificate programmes.

These are general minimums — specific programmes (e.g. Medicine, Engineering) set their own, often higher, cut-off points. Always check the current UB Guide to Prospective Applicants for your intended programme.
What is the government sponsorship cut-off point?
For new students applying for government tertiary sponsorship, the standard mainstream cut-off has been 36 points. A reduced cut-off of 31 points applies to the special dispensation category — orphans, students with special educational needs, and students from remote areas.

These figures are set by the Ministry of Education and Skills Development and can change between sponsorship cycles — confirm current figures with DTEF before relying on them.
What points do you need for BIUST?
Botswana International University of Science & Technology (BIUST) requires a credit (grade C or better) in at least six BGCSE subjects, including a minimum Pass (D) in English and at least a Credit (C) in Mathematics plus two Science subjects. For science-based programmes, BIUST also typically requires a minimum combined score of 38 points across Mathematics, English, and two Science subjects (or Science Double Award).

Entry requirements vary by programme — always confirm with BIUST's current admissions guide.
When are BGCSE and JCE results released in 2026?
BGCSE results for the October–November exam sitting are typically released by BEC in January or February of the following year. JCE follows a similar annual cycle. BEC announces the exact release date publicly each year — check bec.co.bw or BEC's official social media for the confirmed 2026 date, as historically there has been no fixed calendar date.
How do I check my BGCSE or JCE results?
Online: Go to bec.co.bw, select your examination (BGCSE/JCE/PSLE), enter your centre number (e.g. BW800), candidate number, and surname, then click "Show Result."

By SMS: For BGCSE, send your centre number, candidate number and surname to 16688. For JCE, include the prefix JCE before your centre number in the same format. Standard SMS rates in Pula apply.
