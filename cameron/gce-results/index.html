How to check your GCE results

There is no official GCE Board portal where you type in a candidate number and see your personal result instantly online. Results are released as a set of PDF documents, organised by examination centre, on the Board's official site. On release day, the standard ways to find out your result are:

At your centre
Results are sent to the centre where you registered. This is the official route — your school or centre will have the full list and can confirm your individual result.
On the radio
Results are traditionally read out on national and regional radio stations shortly after release — a long-standing way Cameroonian candidates and families get same-day news before written slips are distributed.
Official PDF
The Board publishes the complete results as downloadable PDFs on camgceb.org, broken down by region, division, and centre. This is the authoritative source if you want to verify a result yourself.
Certificate collection
Physical certificates are issued roughly six months after results are published, distributed to Chiefs of Centres for candidates to collect in person (with valid ID for external candidates).
A note on third-party "results checker" apps and sites: a few unofficial tools let you search results by scraping the Board's published PDFs after release. They can be a convenient shortcut, but they are not run by the GCE Board and results should always be confirmed against your official centre slip or the camgceb.org PDF before you make decisions (resits, applications, etc.).

What GCE grades mean

Each subject you sit is graded independently on a letter scale. There is no single combined "average" the way there is for the French-system Baccalauréat in other African countries — your GCE result is a set of individual subject grades, and what matters for certificates and admissions is how many of those are passes.

O Level: you generally need passes in five or more subjects — including English Language and Mathematics — to obtain the GCE O Level certificate.

A Level: each subject is graded on its own, and you can resit individual subjects without retaking the whole exam. Most university faculties ask for at least two relevant principal passes (grades A–E) for admission.

What to do after your results

If you passed: gather your grade slip, birth certificate, and application forms for the university or programme you're targeting — University of Buea, University of Yaoundé I, University of Douala, and others each publish their own minimum subject and grade requirements, so check those against your actual grades before applying.

If you didn't pass a subject: a failed grade doesn't cost you the whole certificate. Register for a resit of just that subject through your school or the GCE Board for the next session, and use past papers to target the specific paper or topic that let you down.

If you're not sure your result is correct: the Board has a formal confirmation-of-results process for candidates who want their script rechecked — this goes through the official GCE Board channel, not third-party apps.

Frequently asked questions

When will GCE results 2026 be released?
The Board hasn't announced an official 2026 date yet. Recent years give a rough pattern: 2025 results were released on 1st August, and 2020 results were released on 2nd October — so the window has varied by a couple of months year to year. The only reliable date is the one the Board itself publishes on camgceb.org closer to release.
Can I check my GCE result online by candidate number?
Not directly through the official Board site — there's no built-in "enter your number, see your result" search on camgceb.org. The Board publishes results as PDFs by centre, and verification is officially done at your registration centre. A few unofficial apps scrape the released PDF to offer a search box, but always cross-check against your official slip.
Where can I find GCE past questions?
Past papers are the single best resit-prep resource — practising the actual paper format and timing matters more than generic revision. Several free and paid repositories host them by subject and level; always check that a source is reasonably recent, since the GCE syllabus and paper format have shifted over the years.
What's the difference between GCE O Level and A Level?
O Level is taken after the 5-year lower secondary cycle in the Anglophone system; A Level follows two further years of upper secondary and is the qualification used for university entry. A Level subjects are graded individually, and 2+ principal passes are typically the baseline for admission consideration.
I failed one subject — do I lose my whole certificate?
No. Each subject stands on its own. You keep the passes you already have and can resit only the subject(s) you failed in a later session, through your school or directly with the GCE Board.
